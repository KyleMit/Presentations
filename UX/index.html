Navigation

  • Path - Set of Steps needed to complete a task
  • Step - Physical and Mental Effort
    • Length - Amount of Time to complete
    • Width - Amount of Difficulty to complete
The Science of Great Design by Mark Miller
Notes:
  • Difficulty increases with precision - if you have a tiny checkbox or a tiny slider handle on mobile, these can crease difficulty

Path Example

ex. Mountain Biking
  • Load Bike on Car
  • Drive to Kingdom Trails
  • Bike up and down course
Notes:
  • Different steps are different context shifts
  • Steps have a length and width property
  • Length of drive might make path harder
  • Biking might have periods of varying difficulty over tiny bridges or paths or logs
  • Kingdom Trails, East Burke, VT

How to Make a Path Easier:

  • Shorten Steps
  • Widen Steps
  • Reduce # of Steps
  • Alternative Steps
Notes:
  • Alternatively, how to make it harder - "Are you sure you want to delete?"
